A Poison Tree

I was angry with my friend;
I told my wrath, my wrath did end.
I was angry with my foe:
I told it not, my wrath did grow.

And I waterd it in fears,
Night & morning with my tears:
And I sunned it with smiles,
And with soft deceitful wiles.

And it grew both day and night.
Till it bore an apple bright.
And my foe beheld it shine,
And he knew that it was mine.

And into my garden stole,
When the night had veild the pole;
In the morning glad I see;
My foe outstretched beneath the tree.

— William Blake

Visualization Notes

This visualization interprets Blake's powerful allegory through dynamic elements that embody the poem's themes of anger, growth, and revenge:

Visual Elements:

  • The Tree: Represents the growing wrath, with branches that extend and leaves that multiply over time
  • Apples: Symbolize the poisonous fruit of anger, gradually changing color as they become more toxic
  • Day/Night Cycle: Represents the passage of time and the nurturing of wrath "both day and night"
  • Shadow: Represents the presence of the foe, growing darker as the tree matures
  • Leaves: Symbolize the "soft deceitful wiles" used to nurture the anger

Interactive Features:

  • Click to toggle between day and night, representing the cycle of nurturing wrath
  • Watch as the tree grows and bears its poisonous fruit
  • Observe how the shadow grows darker as the tree matures
  • See the apples gradually change color as they become more toxic

Visual Metaphors:

  • The tree's growth represents the nurturing and development of anger
  • The changing colors of the apples symbolize the transformation of anger into poison
  • The day/night cycle illustrates the constant attention given to nurturing wrath
  • The growing shadow suggests the increasing presence of the foe
  • The swaying branches and leaves represent the deceptive nature of nurtured anger
